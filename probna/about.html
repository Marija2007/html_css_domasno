Who Am I

My name is Marija Petkova, I’m 18 years old and I come from Valandovo, North Macedonia. I study at a vocational high school in the field of Computer Technology and Automation, and I dream of continuing my education abroad. My plan is to start university in Slovenia, and later pursue a master’s degree in countries like Spain, Italy, the Netherlands, Denmark, Sweden, or Finland.My biggest goal is to work as a programmer, build a beautiful life, and travel the world. Technology excites me, but music is what keeps me grounded. Wherever I go, music follows me—it’s my constant companion. I listen everywhere, and one day I hope to learn how to play the piano to express that passion even more deeply. I’m also part of Semos Education, where I’m learning Fullstack JavaScript development. This is where my technical journey begins, combining my love for problem‑solving with creativity.

My Musical Journey

Music has always been more than background noise for me—it’s the soundtrack of my life. From the quiet streets of Valandovo to the crowded energy of concerts in Skopje, every song I’ve listened to has shaped who I am and how I feel. I grew up with headphones on, discovering how different genres could change my mood: pop songs lifting me up, ballads helping me process sadness, and trap beats giving me confidence. Over time, I realized that music isn’t just entertainment—it’s a way of understanding myself. Concerts became milestones in this journey. Singing and dancing with friends at Jala & Buba’s show in Skopje reminded me how powerful music can be when shared. It’s not just about the artists—it’s about the connection between thousands of voices echoing the same lyrics. Looking ahead, I dream of learning to play piano, so I can not only listen but also create. Music will always travel with me—whether I’m coding, studying abroad, or exploring new cities. It’s the one constant in a world full of change.
