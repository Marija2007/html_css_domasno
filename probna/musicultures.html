Sounds from Around the World

Music is a universal language, but every country speaks it differently.

  • Latin America : Reggaeton and bachata (Ozuna, Maluma, Prince Royce) bring rhythm, dance, and passion.

  • Balkan Region : Trap and pop-folk (Jala & Buba, Senidah) mix tradition with modern beats, creating a unique cultural identity.

  • Asia : K-pop (BTS) blends polished production with emotional storytelling, reaching global audiences.

  • Western Pop/R&B : Ariana Grande, The Weeknd, Chase Atlantic and The Neighbourhood represent modern pop culture, mixing vulnerability with empowerment.

  • Ex-Yu Classics : Tose Proeski, Zdravko Colic and Saša Kovačević show how timeless ballads carry emotion across generations.

Traditional vs Modern

This section can compare how music evolves but still keeps its emotional roots.

  • Traditional : Folk songs, ballads, and ex-Yu classics often focus on storytelling, love, and nostalgia. They carry cultural heritage and connect generations.

  • Modern : Trap, reggaeton, and K-pop are built on beats, energy, and global trends. They reflect today’s fast lifestyle, digital culture, and youth identity.

  • Bridge Between Them : Artists like Senidah or Jala & Buba take traditional Balkan influences and fuse them with modern trap, proving that old and new can coexist.
