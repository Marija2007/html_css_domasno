  • Top 5 Songs That Inspire Me

    1. Te Boté – Nio García, Darell, Casper Mágico (Remix ft. Bad Bunny, Ozuna, Nicky Jam)
      For me, “Te Boté” is not only about ending a toxic relationship—it also reflects the silent pain of unspoken feelings. The line “paso mucha noche pensándote” reminds me of those nights spent thinking about someone I care for deeply, even though we never talk. Sometimes all we share is a glance, a moment of eye contact, and nothing more. This song captures that bittersweet mix of longing and distance, showing how music can express emotions that words often fail to convey.

    2. Swim - Chase Atlantic
      3. For me, “Swim” feels like the soundtrack of silent connections. The hypnotic rhythm mirrors the tension between two people who recognize each other, yet remain distant—like strangers. The line “exercising demons” becomes a metaphor for the unspoken battles inside, while the watery imagery reflects the way emotions flow beneath the surface. No words, no touch, just the weight of what could be. The song inspires me because it captures the beauty and pain of unspoken feelings, reminding me that sometimes silence speaks louder than conversation.

    3. Reflections – The Neighbourhood
      4. “Reflections” feels like a song about surrendering to a gaze. The lyric “I see my reflection in your eyes” becomes even more powerful when a woman is losing herself in the deep blue eyes of a man. It’s not about strangers or distance—it’s about intensity, about the way one look can dissolve boundaries and make you forget the world around you. The dreamy, melancholic sound mirrors that moment of vulnerability, when emotions overflow and you realize that someone else holds a mirror to your soul. For me, “Reflections” is inspiring because it shows how music can capture the beauty of being seen, truly seen, in another person’s eyes.

    4. When I Was Your Man – Bruno Mars
      5. There are breakups that happen without a real reason—no betrayal, no fading love—just fear, insecurities, and lies that slowly push two people apart. “When I Was Your Man” feels like the soundtrack to that kind of loss. Bruno Mars sings with raw honesty, confessing the regret of not holding on, of letting love slip away when it could have been saved. The piano is bare, almost fragile, mirroring the vulnerability of a man who realizes too late what he had. Listening to this song is like reading a letter never sent: a plea for forgiveness, a wish to turn back time, and the haunting thought of what she might be doing now, somewhere out there, living a life that no longer includes him. It’s inspiring because it reminds us that love should never be taken for granted, and that silence, fear, and half-truths can destroy what was once whole.

    5. 12 to 12 - sombr
      6. This song is the sound of obsession wrapped in melancholy. Sombr sings about the kind of love that refuses substitutes: “In a room full of people, I look for you.” It’s not that others aren’t there, it’s that none of them feel like him. Even though he is flawed, even though he is not shining or perfect, the heart still chooses him. The track captures the paradox of love—wanting someone despite their darkness, despite the lies or insecurities that made them impossible to hold on to. It’s a confession of vulnerability, of searching for one person in every crowd, and admitting that no one else can take their place. For me, “12 to 12” is inspiring because it shows how love can be irrational yet deeply human. It reminds us that sometimes we cling to the imperfect, because in their imperfection we found something irreplaceable.
  • Album Review : (Dangerous Woman – Ariana Grande)

    1. Dangerous Woman is not just an album—it’s a declaration. Released in 2016, it marks the moment Ariana Grande transformed from rising star into a fearless pop powerhouse. Every track feels like a different shade of desire, empowerment, and playful provocation, making the album a journey through passion and strength.

    2. Into You

      3. This is the heartbeat of the album. Into You is pure adrenaline, a song that feels like falling in love with danger itself. It’s explosive, intoxicating, and impossible to resist. Listening to it is like being swept into a storm—you know it’s risky, but you can’t stop yourself.

    3. Dangerous Woman

      4. The title track is the soul of the record. It’s about embracing challenges, temptations, and the parts of yourself that refuse to be tamed. Dangerous Woman is seductive yet defiant, a song that makes you feel powerful even in vulnerability. It’s the anthem of confidence, the moment Ariana claims her identity without apology.

    4. Side to Side

      5. Here comes the flirtation. With its reggae-inspired rhythm and cheeky lyrics, Side to Side is playful, mischievous, and irresistibly fun. It’s Ariana at her most daring, turning attraction into a game, teasing with every beat.

    5. Why the Album Matters

      6. Dangerous Woman is the album that challenged everything—it’s passion (Into You), empowerment (Dangerous Woman), and flirtation (Side to Side). Together, these songs show Ariana’s range: she can be vulnerable, bold, and playful all at once. It’s the record that proved she wasn’t just another pop singer, but an artist who could own her narrative and redefine her sound.
  • Concert Diary: Jala & Buba in Skopje

    • Jala Brat and Buba Corelli, the trap kings from Sarajevo, kicked off their GOAT Tour right here in North Macedonia—and Skopje was the perfect place to start. The Sports Center “Jane Sandanski” was packed, the lights were flashing, and the energy was unstoppable. I went with my friends—both girls and guys—and from the first beat we knew it would be a night to remember. We sang every lyric, we danced until our voices cracked, and the vibe was pure fire. The crowd was one massive family, united by the music. When “Klinka” started, the arena shook. Everyone screamed the chorus louder than the speakers, and it felt like the whole city was echoing. Songs like “Ona”, “Ne Volim”, and “Mila” kept the energy high, but Klinka was the moment that defined the night. Everything was top—performance, atmosphere, and emotion. Walking out after midnight, we were still buzzing, carrying the beats with us through the streets of Skopje. It wasn’t just a concert; it was a celebration of friendship, music, and belonging.
  • Music and Emotions : How Songs Shape My Mood

    • Music is more than sound—it’s a mirror of my emotions. Every song I listen to has the power to shift my mood, to color my day in unexpected ways.

    • Passion: A fiery reggaeton track like Te Boté makes me feel unstoppable, as if I’m dancing through life with confidence.

    • Depth: Atmospheric songs like Swim pull me inward, reminding me of the vastness of thought and feeling.

    • Reflection: Tracks like Reflections slow me down, helping me see myself more clearly.

    • Regret: Ballads like When I Was Your Man bring vulnerability to the surface, letting me process loss and longing.

    • Longing: 12 to 12 by Sombr captures obsession and insecurity, echoing the ache of searching for someone who isn’t there.

    Each song is like a key that unlocks a different emotional room inside me. Sometimes I choose music to match my mood, other times to change it—but either way, it shapes the way I move through the world.
